Bitcoin Fluctuation Events

Mt Gox Hack

In June 2011, Mt. Gox suffered its first hack when hackers manipulated the price of bitcoin to 1 cent. They acquired around 2,000 bitcoin by using customers' private keys. Additionally, customers purchased 650 bitcoin, but none of it was returned. Investigations later revealed that Mt. Gox's private key had been stolen in 2011, though it remains unclear whether it was obtained through hacking or with insider assistance.

The Covid 19

The COVID-19 pandemic, caused by the SARS-CoV-2 virus, is a global outbreak of the coronavirus disease 2019. It originated in Wuhan, China, in December 2019 and quickly spread across Asia and eventually worldwide. The World Health Organization declared it a public health emergency of international concern on January 30, 2020, and later classified it as a pandemic on March 11, 2020. As of June 7, 2023, there have been 767,364,119 confirmed cases and 6,938,340 confirmed deaths, making it one of the deadliest pandemics in history.

Russia-Ukraine war

In February 2022, Russia invaded and occupied parts of Ukraine, escalating the ongoing Russo-Ukrainian War that started in 2014. The invasion has caused a significant loss of life on both sides and triggered the largest refugee crisis in Europe since World War II. By June, around 8 million Ukrainians were internally displaced, and by May 2023, over 8.2 million had fled the country.
